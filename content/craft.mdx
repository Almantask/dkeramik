# The Craft: From Clay to Home

## How Each Piece Comes to Life

Creating ceramic pieces is a slow conversation between maker and material. It cannot be rushed. Here's the journey each piece takes from raw earth to your hands.

---

## 1. **Selecting the Clay**

It begins with the right clay body. I work primarily with **stoneware**—strong, durable, with a beautiful natural texture. Sometimes porcelain, when a piece calls for delicacy. The clay arrives in dense blocks, cool and ready, holding infinite possibility.

*The choice of clay determines everything: how the piece will feel in your hands, how it will respond to glaze, how it will age with time.*

---

## 2. **Centering**

The first and most meditative step. A lump of clay on the wheel, spinning. My hands apply steady pressure, coaxing it into perfect alignment. When the clay is truly centered, there's a moment of stillness even as it spins—a kind of calm focus.

This is where presence matters most.

---

## 3. **Shaping**

With the clay centered, the form begins to emerge. Walls rise under gentle pressure. A bowl opens wide. A vase reaches upward. Each piece is shaped by feel as much as sight—the thickness of the wall, the curve of the rim, the weight and balance.

**No two pieces are identical.** The hand leaves its trace.

---

## 4. **Trimming & Refining**

Once the piece has dried to leather-hard, it returns to the wheel. I trim away excess clay from the base, refining the foot, creating clean lines. This is detail work—patient, precise. The piece takes its final form.

---

## 5. **Bisque Firing**

The first fire. Pieces are loaded into the kiln and slowly brought to 1800°F. This transforms the clay from something fragile into something permanent. The chemical change is irreversible. What was earth becomes ceramic.

---

## 6. **Glazing**

Now comes color and surface. I mix glazes in the studio—earthy tones, soft creams, warm terracottas. Each piece is dipped, brushed, or poured with glaze. At this stage, the colors look nothing like they will after firing. It requires trust.

---

## 7. **Glaze Firing**

The final transformation. The kiln reaches 2200°F. The glazes melt, flow, and fuse to the clay. What emerges is glossy or matte, smooth or textured, **always slightly surprising**. Even after years of practice, each kiln opening holds magic.

---

## 8. **Into Your Hands**

After cooling, each piece is inspected. Feet are sanded smooth. The piece is washed, photographed, and carefully wrapped. Then it travels to you—ready to become part of your daily rituals, your home, your life.

---

*Every piece carries the warmth of fire and the touch of hands.*
