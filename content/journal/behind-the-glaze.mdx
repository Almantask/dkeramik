# Behind the Glaze: What Happens in the Workshop

Every morning, when I arrive at the workshop, the first thing I do is touch the clay. It's like a greeting. The clay is cold, damp, full of possibilities.

## What You Don't See

Most people only see the finished piece — beautiful, glazed, ready. But the real magic happens between the stages: when the clay cracks and you must start over, when the glaze surprises you with an unexpected colour, when the kiln temperature decides everything.

**The workshop is a place of happy accidents.** Some of my favourite pieces were born from mistakes — a glaze that ran differently than expected, a form that shifted on the wheel and became something I never planned.

## The Language of Hands

There are moments when hands know more than the mind. When a piece chooses its own form. The clay has memory — it remembers every touch, every hesitation, every confident stroke.

Working on the wheel is a conversation. I suggest, the clay responds. Sometimes it agrees. Sometimes it has other ideas. **The best pieces come from listening.**

## Patience as Practice

Ceramics teaches patience. It teaches you to accept imperfection as a gift. And most importantly — it teaches that creation is a journey, not a destination.

You cannot rush clay. You cannot skip the drying. You cannot control the kiln. At some point, you learn to let go and trust the process.

## Behind Closed Doors

Today I wanted to share what stays behind the closed workshop doors. The clay dust on every surface. The stack of pieces waiting for their turn in the kiln. The quiet satisfaction of a morning spent shaping something from nothing.

This is the life behind the glaze — unglamorous, meditative, and deeply fulfilling.
