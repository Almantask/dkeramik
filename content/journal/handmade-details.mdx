# Handmade Details: Building a Home That Feels Like You

I once visited someone's apartment that looked like a magazine spread. Everything matched. Everything was perfectly styled. It was beautiful.

It also felt like a hotel.

## The Difference Between Decorated and Lived-In

There's nothing wrong with a beautifully decorated space. But there's a difference between a space that's been **designed** and a space that's been **lived in** with intention.

The difference is in the details:

- The **mismatched bowls** that work together anyway
- The **handmade mug** from a local potter
- The **small vase** holding grocery store flowers
- The **throw blanket** your grandmother made
- The **cutting board** worn smooth from use

These aren't perfect. They're **personal**.

## Curating vs. Accumulating

Building a home with handmade details isn't about buying everything handmade. It's about **choosing thoughtfully**.

Ask yourself:

- *Does this object serve a purpose?*
- *Does it bring me joy to use or see it?*
- *Does it tell a story I want to remember?*

If the answer to at least one is yes, it belongs in your home.

## The Mix Matters

Some of the most beautiful kitchens I've seen have:

- **Vintage plates** from thrift stores
- **Handmade bowls** from local makers
- **Inherited pieces** from family
- **A few new items** that tie everything together

The mix is what makes it interesting. The imperfection is what makes it **yours**.

## Living With Your Things

Here's the secret: **beautiful things are meant to be used.**

That handmade bowl? Use it daily, not just for special occasions. That delicate cup? Let it hold your everyday tea. That vase? Keep it filled, even with weeds from the yard.

The more you use beautiful things, the more beautiful your daily life becomes. Simple as that.

## Small Changes, Big Impact

You don't need to replace everything. Start with one category:

- **Dinner bowls**: Replace your mismatched plastic with handmade ceramic
- **Coffee mugs**: Find one perfect one and actually use it
- **Serving pieces**: Invest in a beautiful platter that makes weeknight dinner feel special
- **Small vases**: So you can bring nature inside, one stem at a time

One change ripples outward. Suddenly you're paying more attention to the meal. To the flowers. To the moment.

## The Feeling of Home

What makes a space feel like home? It's not the size or the finishes or the furniture. It's the **small details** you interact with every day.

The mug you reach for first. The bowl that fits your hands. The vase on the windowsill catching afternoon light.

**Homes are created little by little—detail by detail.**
